Recognising your habits

The first step in order to build better habits to to first recognise them. A good method of completing this is by going through a regular day and listing down each habit that you have, big or small. Your lists of habits could end up looking like this:

Secondly, you mark if your habits are positive, netural, or negative. We can classify these as:

  • Positive - Habits that benefit you in the long run.
  • Neutral - Habits that are negligible in the long run.
  • Negative - Habits which are harmful in the long run.

The question is, how can we apply this to learning information security (infosec)?

Harnessing your habits

In order to learn effectively, we should make it a habit to learn about topics in infosec and build habits that enable us to do this. An effective way of doing this, is to make learning, or learning about hacking/cyber security/infosec a part of our identity. This is because you change your attitude from this is what I want to this is what I am as a person.

Other helpful ways of building habits include:

  • Create a concrete plan of action. That uses a behavior, time and location. e.g. I want to work through 30mins of a Hack The Box academy course after breakfast
  • Build new habits, by linking them to old habits. e.g. When I'm drinking my tea, I'm going to listen to Darknet Diaries.
  • Optimise your computer setup and make it easy to learn. e.g. Create a bookmark toolbar that has links to your learning resources.
